<p><strong>The Preclinical Brain Imaging Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Computed Tomography (CT)</li><li>Positron Emission Tomography (PET)</li><li>Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI)</li><li>Single Photon Emission Computed Tomography (SPECT)</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Preclinical brain imaging encompasses various advanced technologies used to visualize neurological structures and functions in research settings. Computed Tomography (CT) provides detailed cross-sectional images using X-rays. Positron Emission Tomography (PET) detects metabolic processes via radioactive tracers. Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) offers high-resolution images based on magnetic fields and radio waves, enabling soft tissue visualization. Single Photon Emission Computed Tomography (SPECT) evaluates blood flow and metabolic activity using gamma rays. Other methods may include ultrasound or optical imaging techniques, further enhancing research capabilities.</p></p>
